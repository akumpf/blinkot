blin·kot  (blĭng′kət) n.

abbr: weB LINK chariOT.

1. a vehicle for sharing short-form, curated web content.

2. a decentralized, democratized, and robust way to collect and distribute concise information.

3. anagram of "inkblot"; an invitation to see things as you wish.

Content from anywhere.
Blinkots can combine content from multiple sources such as YouTube, Vimeo, and SoundCloud into one sharable page.
Editable by everyone.
Blinkots ensure that every user can easily modify and fork existing content for quick updates and simple sharing.
Decentralized storage.
Each blinkot's content is contained entirely within the URL and doesn't belong to any particular site or service.
Lightweight and open.
Blinkot code is extremely small, delivered over CDN, and automatically cached for fast loading. It's also released as open source for anyone to improve upon.

Why blinkot? Why now?

Fact. The internet is enormously huge. But scattered throughout its massive jumble of data are a handful of digital treasures -- videos, images, sounds, stories, and information crafted with great care and detail that make our lives better.

What we increasingly need, in addition to good content, is thoughtful curation. We need well-constructed collections and explanations to reduce the countless hours spent sifting through all the random bits in the cloud. We need a simple way to create order among the chaos.

That's where blinkots come in.

Blinkots enable everyone to curate and connect content referenced anywhere on the web. Think of it as a middleground between a short tweet and a long blog post.

All blinkot data (such as its title and HTML content) is stored entirely in its URL; no userdata or content is ever sent or stored on any blinkot server. That means there's no cost to letting everyone edit and fork blinkots, enabling them to scale in incredible ways.

It's worth noting that blinkots can lead to fairly long URLs that may seem scary at first, so a 3rd-party URL shortener (such as goo.gl, which can be used when you press the Share button) makes life a lot easier. If you'd like to bypass the URL shortener for any reason, just copy the link directly from your browser's navigation bar and use that instead.
